9. How is Cell Padding different form cell Spacing? With example 9. Cellpadding and cellspacing are two attributes of HTML tables that are used to control the amount of space between the cells and the content within individual cells. Cellpadding is used to define the space between the border of a cell and its content. Cellspacing is used to define the space between adjacent cells in a table. For example, if you set cellpadding=5 and cellspacing=10, then each cell will have 5 pixels of padding around its content and there will be 10 pixels of space between adjacent cells

11. What is a different between a block-level element and an inline element? 11. In HTML, block-level elements are those that take up the full width available and always start on a new line. They create large blocks of content like paragraphs or page divisions. On the other hand, inline elements do not start on a new line and only take up as much width as necessary. They are used for small bits of content that appear within a block-level element.

18. Wt is SVG? 18. SVG stands for Scalable Vector Graphics. It is a vector-based graphics format that defines graphics in XML format1. SVG images can be created and edited with any text editor, and they can be searched, indexed, scripted, and compressed. They are also scalable, meaning they can be printed with high quality at any resolution and do not lose any quality if they are zoomed or resized.
20. What are logical and physical tags in HTML? 20. In HTML, logical tags describe the meaning or structure of the content it surrounds, while physical tags describe the presentation or appearance of the content . Logical tags are also known as structural tags and present the functions of text on the page . Physical tags are used to provide the visual appearance to the text . Logical tags are used only to give information about the text, written in the HTML page.
